Who Are We?

Mark Skillin

Teaching Elder

Mark was apprehended by the grace of God in 1987 when staff members of Campus Crusade and the Navigators at the University of Colorado shared the message of the Gospel with him. He accepted Jesus Christ as his Lord and Savior and, by God's grace, has never looked back. He lives in Fall River, Massachusetts with his wife, Lynne, and their children, Jacob, Hannah, and Sarah, where in addition to pastoring the church, he works for FedEx.

Mick Yokel

Shepherding Elder

Mick was led to Christ in 1984 through reading C.S. Lewis' Mere Christianity. Since that time he has continued by God's grace to hold fast to the faith. Throughout his Christian walk he has been privileged to served both as an elder and a deacon in various churches. He lives in Fall River, Massachusetts with his wife, Jeannine, and their two grown sons, Chris and Marc. Outside of his church service he works for the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

Chris Yokel

Worship Leader and Teacher

Chris was blessed to grow up from birth in a Christian home, and was baptized at the age of 13. He has been involved in worship ministry, starting as a background singer, then becoming a instrumental accompanist, and finally a regular worship leader. Chris leads in worship, and assists in preparing the liturgy. He currently teaches writing and literature at several local colleges.

Statement of Faith

  1. We believe that the Holy Scriptures as found in the original manuscripts,
    are the inspired and infallible revelation of God to man. The Holy Scriptures consist of the sixty-six books of the Old and the New Testaments, which were written by holy men and prophets of old as they were led by the Holy Spirit. In them, God reveals His covenant, wherein instructions for all man's worship and living are either expressly written or reasonably inferred. We believe that only by the Holy Spirit's regeneration can man come to a saving knowledge of God through Scripture, and only by the Spirit's illumination can he fully understand its truths. We also believe that in all controversies, the Scriptures must be our final judge, so that God alone rules His church and His people through His Word.
  2. We believe that there is one God who created the heavens and the earth,
    who exists in three eternally united yet distinct persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.
  3. We believe that the Son is eternally begotten of the Father,
    and that for us and our salvation He became man, being conceived by the power of the Holy Spirit and was born of the virgin Mary. He lived a life of suffering, and was crucified and buried, and on the third day He was from the dead. He ascended to heaven and is seated at the right hand of the Father and will return again to judge both the living and the dead.
  4. We believe that Christ's kingdom was inaugurated through His ministry,
    and He now rules with all authority in heaven and will bring His kingdom to consummation with His second coming. The Good News or the Gospel is the proclamation of Christ's Lordship and all that Christ's Lordship entails, not least is the receiving of God's sole provision of reconciliation through the death of Jesus Christ for our sins.
  5. We believe that Christ's death on the cross was substitutionary.
    This means that our sins are imputed to Christ and atoned for on the cross. We further believe that Christ's death was both propitiatory and expiatory. By propitiatory, we mean that Jesus' death appeases the Father's wrath against our sin and rebellion. By expiatory we mean that the guilt of our sin is removed by Christ's sacrificial death. We also believe that apart from the reception of pardon through Christ's atoning death, that there is no reconciliation to God.
  6. We believe that we gain the benefits of Christ's sacrificial death by a persevering faith in all that God has graciously promised,
    and by persevering faithfulness to all that God has graciously commanded us through Jesus Christ and His inspired apostles in the New Covenant. We believe that faith in all God has promised through Christ is the means by which we obey all that God has commanded through Christ. We further believe that both faith in the promises of God and obedience to the commands of God are enabled by the grace of God, as man is unable to respond of his own accord to the "good news" of God's saving provision.
  7. We believe that Jesus Christ will return to judge the living and the dead.
    By this we mean that Jesus will return to vindicate and reward with eternal life all those who have overcome and remained faithful to Him through His gracious covenant and to condemn all those who have rejected Him and failed to overcome, stand firm, and remain faithful to Him through His gracious covenant.
  8. We believe that all those who are rewarded with eternal life will populate the New Heavens and the New Earth
    and will enjoy the blessed presence of God and His renewed creation forever. This renewed creation will be a full restoration of the pre-fall paradise existence at that time being universally and eternally enjoyed. We also believe that those who are condemned will forever be cast out of God's blessed presence and thereby suffer an eternal state of conscious torment in hell.
  9. We believe that the Church is the gathering of believers,
    who come together for instruction and exhortation from the Word of God, for the observation of the church's ordinances of baptism and the Lord's Supper, for worship and fellowship, mutual encouragement and correction, and equipping for service and mission. We believe that the Church was instituted by Jesus Christ as a necessary means of grace by which His people persevere in faith and faithfulness. As His people persevere in faith and faithfulness we are a light to the nations and bear testimony by word and deed to the supremacy of Christ in all things.

Books

Saved by Faithfulness: How the Covenant Shapes Our Understanding of Salvation

By Mark Skillin

Evangelical Christianity has long been founded on the theological tradition of "justification by faith alone." This is the heart of the Protestant gospel. But how close is it to the Gospel revealed in Scripture? In Saved by Faithfulness, Rev. Mark Skillin highlights the interpretational problems that emerge when one holds to a faith alone paradigm. He talks about the importance of the concept of covenant in Scripture, and demonstrates through a survey of the biblical covenants that God has always placed faith and obedience in very close relationship to each other as necessary for the salvation of His people. Saved by Faithfulness challenges centuries of Scriptural interpretation and consequent church practice which have enshrined a distorted gospel, with far reaching consequences for the church. It is a call to return to faithful biblical interpretation and faithful Christian lives.

Saved by the Cross: How the Covenant Reveals the Meaning of the Cross

By Mark Skillin

It may be wondered why another book on the cross of Christ is necessary. Simply put, most writings have limited themselves to the meaning of the cross in terms of the cleansing our sin, apart from covenantal considerations. When the cross is understood in terms of the covenant, in turns out that Christ's cross-work has far more expansive meaning for us than we have traditionally thought. Saved by the Cross seeks to explore this expansive meaning.

Looking Forward to our Justification with the Earliest Church Fathers

Mark Skillin

"But after coming to this conclusion [that works enabled by faith justify us before God], it occurred to me that it would be very interesting to look again, and in some cases for the first time, at the teachings of the earliest church fathers. What I discovered was a pleasant surprise. As it turns out, the earliest apologists and pastors had a remarkably Pauline understanding of salvation. The burden of this article is to show how this is the case. The conception of how and when the early fathers understood they were to be justified before God bears a remarkable and unmistakable similarity to Paul's doctrine. What will be seen is that these modern scholars, whom I have quoted, are in fact correct. Justification by faith alone is absent in the vast majority of early patristic teaching."

A Tale of Two Covenant Systems

Chris Yokel

"Throughout the Bible, covenant is the medium through which God always relates to His people. God has never related to mankind or to His people apart from or outside of a covenant relationship. Covenant then is the atmosphere that we breathe as Christians, and it is the atmosphere that permeates all of Scripture. It is of vital importance then that we properly understand the shape of covenants throughout Scripture, and where that leaves us standing today as people in covenant with the holy God. It is also important how we see the covenants relating to one another, for as we will see, the conclusions that we draw about one often lead to our understanding of the others."
